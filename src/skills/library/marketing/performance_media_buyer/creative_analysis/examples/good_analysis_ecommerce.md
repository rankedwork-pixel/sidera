# Example: E-Commerce Creative Analysis (DTC Brand)

## Scenario
A DTC skincare brand spending $85K/month on Meta, running 14 active
creatives across 3 campaigns (Prospecting, Retargeting, Lookalike).

## What Good Looks Like

### Executive Summary
Creative health is **moderate** — we have 2 clear winners driving 58% of
conversions at well below target CPA, but the bottom 5 creatives are
burning $12K/week with zero backend conversions. The winning creative
mix is dangerously concentrated: "Before/After UGC" accounts for 41% of
all spend. We need to diversify urgently.

### Key Decisions Made

**Scaled (2 creatives):**
- "Before/After UGC v2" — Backend ROAS 6.2x (platform reported 8.1x,
  inflation ratio 1.31). Increased budget 15% ($340/day → $391/day).
  Rationale: Consistent top performer for 21 days, frequency still at
  1.8 (healthy), CTR stable at 2.4%.
- "Founder Story 60s" — Backend ROAS 4.8x. Increased 10%.
  Rationale: Strong hook rate (67% watch past 25%), growing conversion
  volume over last 7 days.

**Cut (3 creatives):**
- "Product Grid Static" — $2,100 spend, 4,200 impressions, 0 backend
  conversions. Platform reported 3 conversions but backend shows zero.
  Weekly savings: $2,100.
- "Lifestyle Carousel v1" — Backend CPA $94 vs target $35. Frequency
  at 4.2 and CTR declining 0.3pp/day for 9 days. Classic fatigue.
- "Testimonial Static v3" — Backend ROAS 0.4x after 12 days and
  6,800 impressions. Sufficient data, clearly underperforming.

**Reduced budget (2 creatives):**
- "Problem/Solution Reel" — Backend ROAS 1.9x (below 2.5x target but
  not disastrous). Reduced from $180/day to $90/day for 7-day test.
  Rationale: Only 890 impressions in first week — insufficient data for
  a hard cut, but early signals are weak.

### Why This Analysis Is Good
1. **Backend vs platform comparison** on every creative (inflation ratio)
2. **Specific metrics** — not "performing well" but "ROAS 6.2x, CTR 2.4%"
3. **Fatigue detection** with quantified decline (0.3pp/day for 9 days)
4. **Conservative cuts** — reduced budget instead of killing a creative
   with only 890 impressions
5. **Concentration risk flagged** — 41% on one creative is a warning
6. **Dollar impact** — "$12K/week savings" not just "cut underperformers"
