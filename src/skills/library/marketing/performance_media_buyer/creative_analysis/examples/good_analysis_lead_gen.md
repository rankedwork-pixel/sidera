# Example: Lead Gen Creative Analysis (B2B SaaS)

## Scenario
A B2B SaaS company spending $42K/month on Meta, targeting marketing
directors. 8 active creatives across Lead Gen and Traffic campaigns.
Goal: qualified demo bookings at <$120 CPA (backend-attributed).

## What Good Looks Like

### Executive Summary
Creative performance is **strong but narrowing**. Our top 3 creatives
deliver 82% of qualified demos at $78 avg backend CPA (35% below
target). However, we've exhausted our "pain point" copy angle — all
3 winners use the same theme. Two new "ROI proof" creatives launched
last week show early promise but need 5 more days of data.

### Key Decisions Made

**Scaled:**
- "CFO Pain Point Video 30s" — 47 qualified demos at $62 backend CPA.
  Platform reported $41 CPA (inflation 1.51x — typical for lead gen
  where Meta over-attributes form fills that don't convert to demos).
  Budget increase: 20%.

**Maintained (watching closely):**
- "ROI Calculator Static" — Launched 4 days ago, 3 demos at $91 CPA.
  Only 2,100 impressions. INSUFFICIENT DATA for a decision — need
  minimum 7 days and 5,000+ impressions for B2B lead gen. Keep running.
- "Case Study Carousel" — 12 demos at $104 CPA. Above average but
  below target ceiling. Frequency at 2.1 and stable. Monitor for
  one more week.

**Cut:**
- "Feature Comparison Infographic" — $3,400 spend, 1 demo ($3,400 CPA).
  Running for 14 days with 8,200 impressions. Enough data: this
  creative doesn't resonate. Platform shows 8 leads but only 1 became
  a qualified demo — a 12.5% lead-to-demo rate vs our account average
  of 34%. The copy likely attracts research-stage visitors, not buyers.

### Test Hypotheses
1. **Social proof video** — Combine the pain point hook from our winner
   with customer testimonial footage. Hypothesis: pain point hook +
   social proof = higher trust in cold audiences. Expected impact:
   maintain CTR while improving lead-to-demo rate by 10-15%.
2. **Contrarian static** — "Stop doing [common practice]" angle.
   Our winners all address pain; test whether challenging conventional
   wisdom drives cheaper attention in a saturated feed.

### Why This Analysis Is Good
1. **Lead gen nuance** — tracks lead-to-demo conversion rate, not just
   leads. Platform-reported leads are often junk in B2B.
2. **Longer evaluation windows** — B2B needs more data than DTC; the
   analysis correctly holds judgment on a 4-day-old creative.
3. **Copy angle analysis** — identifies theme concentration ("all 3
   winners use pain point angle") and suggests diversification.
4. **Attribution skepticism** — inflation ratio of 1.51x is realistic
   for B2B lead gen and correctly flagged.
5. **Test hypotheses are specific** — not "try new creatives" but
   specific hook + format + audience combinations with rationale.
